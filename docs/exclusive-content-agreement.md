# Exclusive Listing Agreement

**Status:** Template. Use only with top-5 landlords (highest-traffic, highest-quality listings). Caps at n=5 by design — exclusivity loses meaning above that.
**Form:** One page, plain English. NOT a binding contract drafted by an attorney — this is a good-faith agreement letter both parties sign. For legally binding contracts, consult a Portland attorney.
**Last reviewed:** 2026-04-26

---

## When to use this

Use after a landlord has been on FindStudioSpace as a Pro subscriber for at least 60 days AND has at least one of the following:
- 50+ views/month on their listing
- 3+ inquiries/month
- High-quality photos and a fully filled-out listing
- A space type that anchors a pillar (e.g., the only cyc wall studio in Portland)

These are the landlords whose absence would hurt the platform. Lock them in.

**Do not** offer this to a landlord who has been on the platform less than 60 days. Earn the relationship first.

---

## What we offer the landlord

In exchange for exclusivity, FindStudioSpace provides:
1. **Free Pro tier for the duration of the agreement** (normal price: $29/month).
2. **Top-of-search placement** in their primary category (above all other listings).
3. **Quarterly content feature** — written profile + photo set published on /blog and shared on social.
4. **Quarterly performance report** with traffic, inquiry, and conversion data.
5. **Right of first refusal** on any new premium placement features we launch (homepage hero, sponsored placements, etc.).
6. **No commission, no referral fees, no booking fees** — they keep 100% of every dollar a renter pays them. Forever, even after the agreement ends.

---

## What we ask in return

The landlord agrees to:
1. **List exclusively on FindStudioSpace** for monthly studio rentals during the term. They may continue listing on Peerspace, Giggster, or similar for hourly/daily bookings — those are different products. The exclusivity is on **monthly creative workspace rentals only**.
2. **Refer renters** they cannot accommodate to FindStudioSpace listings (when reasonable).
3. **Provide one written testimonial** for use on the FindStudioSpace site within the first 90 days.
4. **Allow FindStudioSpace to feature their space** in marketing — photos, content, social posts. They retain full ownership of all photos they took; we get a non-exclusive license to use them in our marketing.
5. **Notify us within 7 days** if they decide to delist or close the space.

---

## Term and termination

- **Term:** 12 months from the date both parties sign.
- **Either party may terminate** at any time with 30 days written notice (email is fine — to redraftedco@gmail.com from the landlord, or to the landlord's listing contact email from us). No reason required, no penalty.
- **At termination,** the landlord keeps all photos and content provided. FindStudioSpace removes the listing from featured placement at the next ISR cycle and reverts to standard placement (unless the listing is also being delisted entirely).
- **At end of term,** either party may renew by mutual written agreement. If neither party objects, the agreement converts to a standard Pro subscription at $29/month with no exclusivity obligations.

---

## What this agreement is NOT

- Not a lease. We are not your tenant or landlord.
- Not a contract for services or compensation. No money changes hands.
- Not exclusive to other use cases (hourly bookings, day-rentals, photo shoots booked outside FindStudioSpace are unaffected).
- Not transferable. If you sell or transfer the space, this agreement does not automatically transfer to the new owner.

## How to send

1. Schedule a 15-minute call with the landlord. Don't send this cold.
2. Walk them through the offer verbally first. Listen to their concerns.
3. Send the agreement as a PDF (export from this markdown) attached to a follow-up email.
4. Use a tool like SignWell, HelloSign, or DocuSign for signatures — or accept a scanned/photographed signature page.
5. Store signed agreements in a private folder. Do not commit signed agreements to this repo.

---

## Cap and scarcity

**Caps at 5 active agreements.** Above 5, exclusivity loses meaning to landlords (no perceived scarcity) and creates operational overhead.

When 5 are active, this template retires until one ends. Update queue status accordingly.
