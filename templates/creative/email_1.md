# Creative cold email — Email 1 (initial)

**Word count target:** 95–115 (body only, excluding signature)
**Tone:** peer founder, working-creative voice, conversational
**Hook:** "you're already listed" + peer-creative angle
**Pitch:** free claim, you control the listing + photos + availability
**Anti-Craigslist line required (per playbook modification 3, creative variant only)**

## Subject variants (5 — randomly assigned at send)

1. saw your {space_type} on findstudiospace.com
2. you're already listed (and I wanted to give you control of it)
3. quick note from a Portland creative
4. found your {space_type} — quick question
5. peer founder note re: your {neighborhood} space

## Body

Hi {first_name},

Found your {space_type} listing while building findstudiospace.com — a Portland-only directory for monthly creative workspace. Wanted to flag that your space is already on the directory (pulled from Craigslist), and claiming it is free.

Different from Craigslist — you control the listing, see who's interested, and it stays up until you say otherwise. Inquiries route to your inbox.

{personalized_detail}

If useful: claim takes 2 minutes — {claim_link}. If not, no worries.

— Taylor
findstudiospace.com
{postal_address}

Reply STOP if you'd rather not hear from me.

## CAN-SPAM checklist

- [x] Sender ID (Taylor + findstudiospace.com)
- [x] Subject non-deceptive (refers to actual listing on the directory)
- [x] Identification as outreach (peer-context opener)
- [x] Physical postal address ({postal_address} placeholder)
- [x] Opt-out (Reply STOP)
- [x] Honor within 10 days (suppression list)

## Notes

- `{personalized_detail}` is **mandatory** for this variant — hand-written from Instagram bio, About page, or one specific listing detail. Generator script must enforce non-empty value before sending creative variant.
- Anti-Craigslist line ("Different from Craigslist…") pre-empts the most likely objection. Do NOT add to office variant — landlords don't compare directories to Craigslist on office space.
- `{space_type}` examples: "art studio", "workshop", "yoga studio", "massage room". Use the actual type from listings.type, lowercased.
- If recipient has multiple listings: use the title of one listing only, not "your listings" plural — feels less surveillance-y.
